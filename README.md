# 📊 EDA Risk Analysis in Mountain Sports (Madrid)

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) project focused on mountain sports
federations in the Community of Madrid. The objective is to analyze the
relationship between user profiles, insurance coverage, and accident
claims, providing insights for risk management and decision-making in
the insurance domain.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🎯 Project Objective

To identify patterns in the frequency and severity of accidents based
on:

-   Federated user profile\
-   Type of insurance coverage\
-   Customer behavior

This analysis supports data-driven strategies for risk management and
insurance optimization.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🧠 Methodology

-   Data cleaning and preprocessing of federated licenses and claims
    datasets\
-   Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand distributions and
    relationships\
-   Statistical analysis and visualization using Python\
-   Hypothesis validation based on observed patterns

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🧪 Hypotheses

-   **Hypothesis A:** Users with higher insurance coverage present
    higher claim rates\
-   **Hypothesis B:** Users over 35 years old present higher average
    claim severity

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 📊 Key Findings

-   Significant differences in claim rates depending on insurance type\
-   Users over 35 years old show a higher proportion of severe
    accidents\
-   Global claim rate is approximately **33 incidents per 1,000
    federated users**\
-   The proposed hypotheses are supported by the exploratory analysis
